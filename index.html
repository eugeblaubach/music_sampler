The Beginning

Fleetwood Mac is a British-American rock band, formed in 1967 by guitarist Peter Green, drummer Mick Fleetwood and guitarist Jeremy Spencer. It began as a no frills blues band, and although the band is commonly associated with powerhouse singer Stevie Nicks, she actually didn’t join the band until 1974.

Fame

In the midst of their fame, the band members experiences emotional turmoil. The album Rumours, released in the spring of 1977, is a testament to their newfound wealth, personal tensions and high intake of drugs and alcohol. The album won the Grammy Award for Album of the Year, generating multiple singles including “Go Your Own Way” and “Dreams.”

Where are they now?

The band was dismantled in 1995, but a year later regrouped to produce a soundtrack for the hit film “Twister.” The band continues to tour and their songs frequently appear in movies and TV shows. They are set to embark on a new tour in 2018.
